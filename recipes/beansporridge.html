Beans Porridge

A nutrient-packed dish mostly eaten as breakfast that can be eaten as is or can be paired with a side of fried plantain, palm oil stew or bread

Ingredients

  • 4 cups Honey beans/ Nigerian brown beans/ Black eyed peas
  • Â¼ cup palm oil
  • 4 scotch bonnet peppers
  • Cameroun Pepper powder
  • 1 Large red onion
  • 1 Large bell pepper
  • 5 tbsp crayfish(optional)
  • Bouillon Cubes
  • Half tablespoon salt

Directions

  1. Rinse the beans and place in a deep pot with 7 cups of water.
  2. Boil the beans on medium heat until it becomes soft, it could take about 40 mins. If you are using black eyed peas, it might take a little longer.
  3. Once the beans are softened from boiling (do not drain the water), turn the heat down to low, add in the crayfish, powdered pepper, salt and bouillon cubes into pot.
  4. Allow the beans to cook for an additional 10 minutes then add in the palm oil. Stir, and allow to cook for another 13 minutes.
  5. Turn off the heat, and allow to sit for 5 minutes before serving

Notes

The beans tend to thicken once they cool. If you prefer your beans lighter, add a little more water and reheat.
